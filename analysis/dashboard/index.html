Suggested Program Order

  • Run reactivation programs on the At risk base second because the recency window is still recoverable.
  • Redesign the first-to-second purchase journey for Newly acquired customers because that is where the long-run value leakage starts.
  • Add a days_since_last_order field for high-value customers and trigger alerts before they age into churn risk.
Experiment Design

Free Shipping Above $100

Task D in Part 1 used a pre/post proxy. For an actual product decision, this should be run as a randomized experiment. The structure below keeps the design causal, focused, and measurable.

Recommended Design

Hypothesis Lift conversion and threshold crossing

Free shipping at $100 should increase completed-order conversion and move more carts from the high-$70s and $90s into the eligible threshold.

Target New Search-led buyers first

Search is the largest acquisition source, while repeat within 90 days is still low. That makes it the cleanest high-scale test cohort.

Primary Metric Completed-order conversion rate

Track the lift in completed orders, not clicks or cart adds. That is the metric closest to incremental gross revenue.

Guardrails Margin, returns, cancellations

Do not ship the change unless conversion lift holds after shipping cost, return rate, and cancellation rate guardrails.

Definitions and Alternatives

How the Dashboard Defines the Core Metrics

These definitions intentionally mirror Part 1 so the static dashboard and the notebook outputs reconcile cleanly.

Primary Definitions

Churn

A customer-month is churned if the user has zero completed orders in the 90 days after the last completed order in that month. Recent months are censored until they have a full 90-day observation window.

Active, New, and Returning

An active customer has at least one completed order in the month. A new customer places their first-ever completed order in that month. A returning customer orders in any later month after that first purchase month.

Revenue and Conversion

Revenue excludes returned items. The conversion rate here is a monthly proxy based on distinct purchase-event users divided by distinct department-event users, not a true session funnel. Because a user can buy in one month and browse a department page in another, the rate can exceed 100% in some months, which happened in this dataset. In a real product setting, I would use session_id plus event sequence.

Alternatives Worth Considering

Alternative churn definitions

For faster intervention, I would also track 30-day reorder churn, 14-day session churn, or cohort-based retention. Those are better for operational product teams than a lagging 90-day purchase definition.

Alternative segmenting logic

If campaign targeting is the priority, I would extend the segments with acquisition channel, country, and price sensitivity. If assortment is the priority, I would add department affinity and return behavior.

Metrics for the shipping test

The primary metric should be completed-order conversion. Key secondary metrics are average order value, revenue per visitor, threshold-crossing share, and second-order rate. Margin, delivery cost, returns, and cancellations are the required guardrails.
